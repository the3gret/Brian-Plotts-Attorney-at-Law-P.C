Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common immigration questions

How long does the green card process take?

Processing times vary significantly depending on the category (family vs. employment), your country of origin, and USCIS backlogs. Most family-based applications take 12-36 months, while employment-based cases typically take 6-24 months. We'll provide a personalized timeline estimate during your consultation.

Can I stay in the U.S. while waiting for my green card?

This depends on your current status and the type of application. Many applicants can remain in the U.S. while their application is pending (called "adjustment of status"), while others may need to complete consular processing abroad. We'll analyze your specific situation to determine the best approach.

What if my visa application was denied?

Denials can often be appealed or overcome with a new application. We'll review the denial notice, identify the reasons, and develop a strategy to address the issues—whether through appeal, motion to reopen, or a fresh application with stronger evidence.

How much does an immigration attorney cost?

Fees vary based on case complexity. We offer transparent pricing with flat fees for most services (like green card applications) and hourly rates for complex litigation. During your consultation, we'll provide a clear fee estimate with no surprises.

Can I apply for citizenship if I have a criminal record?

Some criminal convictions affect naturalization eligibility, while others don't. We'll carefully review your record to determine if you qualify, identify any waivers available, and develop a strategy to present your case in the most favorable light.
